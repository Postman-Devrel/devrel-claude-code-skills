## Postman Logo

Source: https://design.postman.com/brand-foundations/postman-logo/

The Postman brand mark depicts a flying spacehero — a fun, geeky, and meaningful image connected to Postman since 2014, affectionately called the Postmanaut.

**Important:** Do not change the color of the logo or logo mark for any creative projects.

### Sizing for Web

The logotype requires legibility at all times. Never use sizes smaller than what's specified.

### Clear Space

The width of the letter "M" in Postman determines spacing between the logo icon and typeface. Safe area refers to essential white space maintained around the logo in all usage contexts.

### Logo Sizing Standards

- **Max width:** 500–1000px
- **Min width:** 250px
- **Below 250px:** Use icon only

### Available Logo Variants

#### White Logos

| postman-white-stacked-logo | SVG, PNG |
| postman-white-orange-logo | SVG, PNG |
| postman-white-orange-stacked-logo | SVG, PNG |

### General Positioning

Apply these principles to digital and print materials, both portrait and landscape orientations.

- Position logos in one of four corners or centered on the vertical axis
- Maximize logo size while maintaining appropriate spacing
- Be bold, be proud, and make it big!
- Avoid small sizes, as details diminish significantly at tiny scales

### Don'ts

- Do not change the logo colors
- Do not use the logo at sizes smaller than the minimums specified
- Do not place the logo without adequate clear space
- Do not distort, rotate, or alter the logo proportions

## Illustrations

Source: https://design.postman.com/brand-foundations/illustrations/

Narratives and metaphors help people understand how something works; they help envision a world enhanced by a product or service. Illustrations serve as the perfect medium to simplify concepts, engage action, and add delight to the brand. Postman's illustrations set the right context and make the brand engaging.

> **NOTE:** Do not use these illustrations as empty states inside product. Reach out to the design-system team if you need something specific inside the product.

### Illustration Principles

1. **Makes people smile** — Illustrations humanize the product experience; keep them simple and fun.
2. **Doesn't overwhelm or distract** — Product, UI and content take prominence; illustrations should not distract or occupy space that diminishes function.
3. **Tells a story** — Every button, link, and action is part of a narrative flow. Use illustrations to support and visually communicate this story.
4. **Aligns with the brand** — Recognizable characters, consistency in style, recurring elements for brand relevance.
5. **Reference and Simplify** — Think simple metaphors and real-world examples. The strongest concepts are the most simple and relatable ones.

### Illustration Style

**Geometric, Simple, Concise, Interesting**

- Uniform stroke weights and good anatomical structure
- Illustrations are constructed on a 2D plane; depth is conveyed through hierarchy of elements and shadows
- Not all poses work for characters — explore what works within the illustration style
- Make each shape matter; use the fewest shapes and curves as possible
- Ground shadows are pill-shaped to portray flatness; they stem from below the feet — Postmanauts should not appear to stand on boxes
- Background elements do not have a stroke
- Dark shadow is used minimally to add emphasis to objects
- All web illustrations have white fill, enabling use on dark backgrounds
- Gradient orange highlights important features, tools, or brings focus to objects in a scene

### What Illustrations Do

- Simplify complex ideas
- Represent brand persona and voice
- Scale easily and function as modular elements, used whole or in parts
- Speak directly to users
- Help tell stories and engage users
- Add delight to the product
- Work hand in hand with copy — define messaging first
